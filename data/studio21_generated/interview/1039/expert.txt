 
-----Input:----- - First line will contain $T$, number of test cases. $T$ testcases follow :   - Each test case contains two space separated integers $X, Y$.  -----Output:----- For each test case, output an integer denoting minimum number of rounds to clean the lab. -----Constraints----- - $1 \leq T \leq 10^5$ - $ |X|,|Y| \leq 10^9$ -----Sample Input:----- 3 0 5 4 -5 0 10000001 -----Sample Output:----- 1 2 1 -----EXPLANATION:----- -  For the first testcase, you can convert $X$ to $Y$  by choosing $a=5$ and $b=2$. It will cost minimum of $1$ cleaning round. You can select any other combination of $a, b$ satisfying above condition but will take minimum of $1$ cleaning round in any case. -  For the second testcase, you can convert $X$ to $Y$ by choosing $a=1$ and $b=10$. In first round they will replace $X$ to $X+a$ and then in second round replace to $X-b$. You can perform only one operation in one round.
